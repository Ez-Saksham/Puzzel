1. LAYERS OF CIPHER USED

  • Morse Code: Dot = •, Dash = –
    Example: •– = A, ––– = O
  • Pigpen Cipher (Pigeon-style):
    Uses grid-based symbols and dots
    A–I (no dot), J–R (with dot), S–Z (double-box or diagonal)
  • Distractors & Custom Symbols:
    Symbols like %, @, □, | • are RED HERRINGS.
    | • = "switch to Morse code"
  • Wordplay & Misspelling:
    Intentional errors like "STRITKES" or "MITDNIGHT"

2. DECODING STRATEGY (Step-by-step)

  1. Scan for Morse code:
    Example: ••• / –– / •–– → S / M / U
  2. Locate "Switch" markers: | • means switch to Morse
  3. Identify Pigpen blocks via shapes or corners
  4. Ignore distractors like %, @, □
  5. Assemble final decoded phrase:
    STRIKES GOLD AT MIDNIGHT

3. HIDDEN PHRASE BUILD-UP (Deconstruction)

Morse Blocks: S, M, U, T, G, I, R, L
Pigpen Blocks: WATCHING, YOU, etc.
Distractors: Only to mislead and confuse decoders
Repeated Phrases: "STRIKES GOLD AT" – look for timing
